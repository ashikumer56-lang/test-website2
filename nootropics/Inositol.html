What is Inositol?

Inositol (formerly known as Vitamin B8) is a naturally occurring sugar alcohol and glucose isomer. Among its nine stereoisomers, myo-inositol is the most biologically active, accounting for over 90% of total inositol in the human body. It serves a dual role in the brain:

  • Structural: Integral component of phospholipid membranes, maintaining cellular integrity.
  • Functional: Acts as a secondary messenger for multiple neurotransmitters, enhancing intracellular signaling and receptor sensitivity.

While the body synthesizes inositol, dietary sources include citrus fruits, beans, and whole grains.
