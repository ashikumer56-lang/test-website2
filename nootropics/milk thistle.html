What is Milk Thistle?

Milk Thistle, a flowering annual herb native to the Mediterranean, has glossy, variegated leaves and distinctive purple flowers. Standardized extracts yield silymarin, a flavonolignan complex that confers multiple protective properties:

  • Hepatic Regeneration: Stimulates protein synthesis and ribosomal activity in hepatocytes.
  • Antioxidant Boost: Elevates glutathione and scavenges reactive oxygen species in liver and neural tissues.
  • Anti-Inflammatory: Inhibits NF-κB, COX-2, and pro-inflammatory cytokines, facilitating tissue repair.
  • Membrane Stabilization: Prevents lipid peroxidation, preserving cell integrity under toxic stress.
  • Neuroprotective: Blocks β-amyloid aggregation and mitigates excitotoxicity.

History & Traditional Use

First documented in ancient Greek pharmacopeias, Milk Thistle seeds served as remedies for jaundice, liver congestion, and digestive complaints. 16th-century herbal texts expanded its use to gallbladder issues and skin disorders. Modern phytotherapy harnesses isolated silymarin for standardized dosing and evidence-based applications.
