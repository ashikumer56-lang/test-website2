What is N-Acetyl-L-Tyrosine?

NALT is L-Tyrosine with an added acetyl group, increasing its stability and enabling faster transport across the blood-brain barrier. Once in the brain, it converts to L-DOPA—the direct precursor to dopamine—and subsequently to norepinephrine and epinephrine, enhancing alertness, motivation, and stress tolerance.

History & Discovery

Tyrosine was first identified in 1846, and its critical role in neurotransmitter synthesis was elucidated in the mid-20th century. NALT emerged in research during the 1970s as scientists sought more bioavailable tyrosine derivatives. Since then, NALT has been embraced in pharmacology and nootropic circles for its rapid impact on catecholamine levels without requiring large doses.
