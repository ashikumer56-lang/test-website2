What Is Ashwagandha?

Botanical Family: Solanaceae (tomato family)

Native Regions: India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka (now also cultivated in the U.S.)

Active Compounds: Withanolides, sitoindosides, alkaloids

As an adaptogen, Ashwagandha helps your body adapt to stress—mental, physical, or environmental. Rather than stimulating, it steadies your nervous system and shields cells against wear and tear.

Side Effects & Precautions

  • Mild GI Upset: Take with food if sensitive.
  • Drowsiness: Rare—more likely at higher doses or when stacked with sedatives.
  • Thyroid Interaction: Can raise T₃/T₄ levels—monitor if you have thyroid issues.
  • Contraindications: Avoid in pregnancy, autoimmune thyroid disorders, or if scheduled for surgery.
