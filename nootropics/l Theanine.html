What is L-Theanine?

L-Theanine is a unique amino acid found almost exclusively in the leaves of Camellia sinensis (green tea) and certain mushrooms. Discovered in 1949, it’s renowned for promoting relaxation without sedation, making it a cornerstone of traditional tea ceremonies and modern nootropic stacks alike.

Historical & Cultural Background

For over a millennium, Japanese tea masters revered matcha for its ability to sharpen the mind during meditation. They called this state ichibancha—the first brew of serene focus—thanks largely to L-Theanine. Today, tea cultures around the world continue celebrating its calming ritual.
