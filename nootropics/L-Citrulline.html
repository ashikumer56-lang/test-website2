What is L-Citrulline?

L-Citrulline is a non-essential amino acid first isolated from watermelon (Citrullus lanatus) in 1914. Unlike L-Arginine—which suffers from first-pass metabolism—L-Citrulline bypasses the liver and converts to L-Arginine in the kidneys, ensuring a sustainable nitric oxide (NO) boost.

Key roles include:

  • Nitric Oxide Precursor: Enhances vasodilation for improved circulation.
  • Urea Cycle Support: Facilitates ammonia clearance, reducing fatigue.
  • Anti-Inflammatory: Lowers markers like IL-6, aiding recovery.
  • Neurovascular Coupling: Aligns blood flow with brain activity for sharpened focus.
