Key Clinical Studies

  1. Anxiolytic Effects in Animal Models: Rats administered 10 mg/kg Baicalin vs. diazepam and control, then assessed in elevated plus maze. Results: Baicalin produced anxiolysis comparable to low-dose diazepam, without motor impairment. Takeaway: Natural anxiety relief with fewer sedative side effects.
  2. Neuroprotection Against Glutamate Toxicity: Cultured cortical neurons exposed to glutamate insult with or without 20 µM Baicalin. Results: 60% reduction in cell death and preserved mitochondrial function. Takeaway: Prevents excitotoxic damage relevant to stroke and neurodegeneration.
  3. Anti-Inflammatory Action in Microglia: LPS-activated microglial cells treated with 5 µM Baicalin. Results: 50% decrease in pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6). Takeaway: Direct suppression of brain inflammation, supporting cognitive longevity.
  4. Cognitive Enhancement in Aging Mice: Aged mice given 50 mg/kg Baicalin for 8 weeks, then tested on Morris water maze. Results: 30% faster learning curves and improved memory retention vs. controls. Takeaway: Potential to slow age-related memory decline.
  5. BDNF Upregulation in Hippocampal Cells: Hippocampal neuron cultures treated with 15 µM Baicalin for 72 hours. Results: 45% increase in BDNF expression. Takeaway: Underpins cognitive and mood-supporting benefits via enhanced neuroplasticity.
  6. Sleep Quality Improvement in Humans: 50 adults with mild insomnia received 200 mg Baicalin nightly vs. placebo for 4 weeks. Results: Improved sleep onset latency (-20%) and increased total sleep time (+12%). Takeaway: Natural sleep aid that also supports daytime cognition.
