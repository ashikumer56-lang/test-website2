What Is Black Seed Oil?

Black Seed Oil is cold-pressed from the seeds of Nigella sativa, a flowering plant native to southwestern Asia. Long celebrated in Middle Eastern, African, and Asian traditional medicine, it was favored by ancient civilizations—including Egyptians and Greeks—for digestive, respiratory, and skin ailments. Side Effects & Safety

Black Seed Oil is generally regarded as safe for most adults when used at recommended doses. However, consider these precautions:

  • Mild Gastrointestinal Upset: Some users experience nausea, bloating, or mild diarrhea when first starting. Taking with food or switching to standardized softgels typically resolves this.
  • Blood Pressure Reduction: Can lower blood pressure in sensitive individuals. If you’re on antihypertensive medications, monitor your readings and consult your healthcare provider before starting.
  • Blood Sugar Effects: May enhance insulin sensitivity and lower blood glucose levels. Diabetics should track glucose and adjust medications accordingly.
  • Allergic Reactions: Rare cases of contact dermatitis or rash when applied topically. Perform a patch test before widespread application.
  • Potential Drug Interactions: May interact with anticoagulants (e.g., warfarin) and immunosuppressants. Always discuss with your physician if you’re on prescription medications.

Warning: Avoid high-dose Black Seed Oil during pregnancy or breastfeeding without medical supervision. While small culinary amounts are generally safe, therapeutic doses may carry risks.
