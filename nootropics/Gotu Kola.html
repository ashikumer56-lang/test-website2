What is Gotu Kola?

Gotu Kola (Centella Asiatica) is an herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands in Southeast Asia. In Ayurveda, it’s known as “Brahmi” and is celebrated for enhancing memory, supporting mental clarity, and reducing anxiety. Traditional Chinese Medicine also values it for healing skin wounds and calming the spirit. The herb contains potent triterpenoids—asiaticoside, madecassoside, asiatic acid, and madecassic acid—along with flavonoids and essential vitamins that collectively promote neurogenesis, neuroprotection, and balanced neurotransmitter activity.

Side Effects & Warnings

Gotu Kola is generally well-tolerated, but potential adverse effects include:

  • Mild Skin Irritation: Topical use may cause itching or rash in sensitive individuals; discontinue if irritation occurs.
  • Headache & Nausea: Rare at higher doses; split dosage or reduce if symptoms appear.
  • Dizziness & Drowsiness: May occur in some users—avoid operating heavy machinery if affected.
  • Liver Toxicity: High doses over extended periods could stress liver function; monitor liver enzymes if using long-term.

Important: Consult a healthcare provider before use if pregnant, breastfeeding, or on medications—especially sedatives, antihypertensives, or thyroid medications. Discontinue use if any severe adverse reactions occur.
