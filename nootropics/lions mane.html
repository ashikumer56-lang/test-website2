What is Lion’s Mane?

Lion’s Mane (Hericium erinaceus) is a revered medicinal mushroom with cascading white spines resembling a lion’s mane. Historically used in Traditional Chinese Medicine for digestive and cognitive support, modern science highlights its potent neuroregenerative compounds: hericenones in the fruiting body and erinacines in the mycelium.

History & Traditional Uses

Revered in TCM, monks consumed Lion’s Mane to enhance focus and memory during meditation. Japanese longevity practices credit it with protecting gut health and cognitive function. Today, these traditions converge with research uncovering NGF-stimulating effects underpinning its nootropic fame.
