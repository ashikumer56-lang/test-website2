What is Maitake Mushroom?

Maitake, also called "Hen of the Woods," is a polypore fungus native to Asia and North America. Its scientific name, Grifola frondosa, refers to its clustered, frond-like appearance. Traditionally used for diabetes, hypertension, and immune disorders, modern research highlights its cognitive and adaptogenic properties.

  • Beta-Glucans: Polysaccharides that activate macrophages, NK cells, and T-cells for enhanced immunity.
  • Hericenones & Erinacines: Compounds linked to nerve growth factor (NGF) synthesis and neurogenesis.
  • Ergothioneine: A potent antioxidant that crosses the blood-brain barrier.

History & Traditional Use

For centuries in East Asia, Maitake has been used to treat immune deficiencies, digestive ailments, and hypertension. It was consumed as a tonic to promote longevity and vitality. In Japanese folk medicine, Maitake tea is brewed to support metabolic health and general well-being.
