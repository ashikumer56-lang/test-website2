What is Magnolia Bark?

Magnolia Bark is derived from the dried bark of Magnolia officinalis, a flowering tree native to China. Known as Magnoliae Cortex, it contains two primary active constituents:

  • Honokiol: A lipophilic polyphenol with anxiolytic, neuroprotective, and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Magnolol: A structural isomer of honokiol that complements its effects by modulating neurotransmitter pathways.

These compounds act synergistically to support relaxation, mood stability, and cognitive resilience without significant sedation or cognitive dulling.

History & Traditional Use

Magnolia Bark has roots in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) where it is called Houpu. It was historically used to:

  • Treat digestive stagnation, bloating, and abdominal pain.
  • Alleviate stress, irritability, and insomnia.
  • Reduce cough and asthma symptoms through respiratory support.
  • Calm the spirit (Shen) and clear phlegm in TCM theory.

In Japanese Kampo medicine, Magnolia Bark is included in formulations for anxiety and menopausal symptoms, underscoring its adaptogenic versatility.
