What Is Bacopa monnieri?

Botanical Family: Plantaginaceae (formerly Scrophulariaceae)

Native Regions: Wetlands of India and Southeast Asia; now cultivated globally

Active Compounds: Bacosides A & B (saponin glycosides), bacopasaponins, alkaloids

Bacopa monnieri is a creeping aquatic herb whose primary bioactives, the bacosides, readily cross the blood–brain barrier, supporting neuronal function, neurogenesis, and resilience against stress and oxidative damage.
