What is Lemon Balm?

Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) is a perennial herb in the mint family, native to the Mediterranean. Its lemon-scented leaves contain bioactive compounds like rosmarinic acid, rosmaridiphenol, and flavonoids which modulate GABA and acetylcholine pathways—balancing calm relaxation with clear cognition.

Traditionally used in teas, tinctures, and culinary dishes, Lemon Balm’s versatility extends to modern nootropic stacks for stress relief, improved mood, and enhanced memory.

History & Uses Through the Ages

Ancient Greeks crowned beehives with Lemon Balm to attract bees and uplift spirits—hence its genus name Melissa (“honey bee”). Medieval physicians prescribed it for melancholy, insomnia, and digestive distress. Today, herbalists harness its gentle anxiolytic and cognitive benefits across the globe.
