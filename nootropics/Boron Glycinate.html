What Is Boron Glycinate?

Boron Glycinate is a chelated complex of boron—a trace mineral—and the amino acid glycine. Chelation enhances boron’s absorption, ensuring more of the mineral reaches circulation and tissues. While boron isn’t classified as “essential” for human survival, mounting research underscores its vital contributions to:

  • Hormonal Balance: Modulates free testosterone, DHT, and estrogen via SHBG and aromatase pathways.
  • Bone & Joint Health: Supports calcium and magnesium retention, promoting skeletal strength and joint mobility.
  • Inflammation Control: Lowers markers like hs‑CRP, TNF‑α, and IL‑6, reducing systemic inflammation that can impair cognition.
  • Cognitive Support: Indirectly stabilizes neuronal membranes, bolsters antioxidant defenses, and optimizes calcium metabolism.
  • Metabolic Synergy: Enhances magnesium and vitamin D utilization—both critical for neurotransmitter balance and brain energy production.

Side Effects & Safety

Boron Glycinate is generally well-tolerated at standard dosages. However, high or prolonged intake may produce:

  • Nausea or Stomach Upset: Rare and usually mild; taking with food helps mitigate this.
  • Increased Irritability or Aggression: From elevated testosterone and DHT; monitor mood and adjust dose if needed.
  • Hormonal Acne: Possible in individuals prone to breakouts when testosterone rises.

Warning: Avoid megadosing (>20 mg/day) without medical supervision. Those with kidney issues or on hormone‐related medications should consult a healthcare professional before use.
